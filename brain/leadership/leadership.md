# Leadership Experience

## Overview

Leadership has been an important part of my journey alongside software development.

I enjoy taking ownership of responsibilities, working with teams, organizing events, and building communities that create opportunities for students.

Through my leadership roles, I've learned that communication, planning, and teamwork are just as important as technical skills when building successful projects and communities.

---

## GeeksforGeeks Student Chapter, LPU

I previously served as the Chief Operating Officer (COO) of the GeeksforGeeks Student Chapter at Lovely Professional University.

My responsibilities included coordinating the team's activities, planning technical initiatives, improving internal operations, and ensuring smooth execution of events.

I worked closely with different teams to help organize technical workshops, coding events, and community activities that encouraged students to learn and participate.

This role helped me improve my organizational skills, decision-making, and ability to manage multiple responsibilities simultaneously.

---

## Student Career Committee (SCC)

I have also served as the Outreach Engagement Officer in the Student Career Committee (School of Computer Science & Engineering).

My primary responsibility was connecting with industry professionals, guest speakers, and organizations to organize career-focused sessions and technical events for students.

This role strengthened my communication, networking, and professional relationship-building skills.

Working with professionals from different companies gave me valuable insight into industry expectations and helped me understand how successful technical events are organized.

---

## Community Building

One aspect of leadership that I enjoy the most is building communities.

I believe that strong technical communities create opportunities for students to learn beyond the classroom through workshops, hackathons, coding competitions, and networking events.

Being involved in student communities allowed me to contribute not only as a developer but also as someone who helps create learning opportunities for others.

---

## Event Management

Throughout my leadership journey, I have been involved in planning and organizing multiple technical events.

My responsibilities often included:

- Coordinating with speakers
- Managing event logistics
- Team coordination
- Student outreach
- Event promotion
- Execution planning
- Communication between different teams

These experiences taught me how important planning and teamwork are for delivering successful events.

---

## Teamwork

I enjoy working with teams because different perspectives often lead to better ideas and better solutions.

Whether I'm building software or organizing an event, I believe collaboration and clear communication are essential.

I'm comfortable taking ownership of tasks while also supporting teammates whenever needed.

---

## Challenges

Leadership comes with challenges.

Managing deadlines, coordinating multiple people, handling unexpected issues during events, and balancing technical work with leadership responsibilities taught me how to remain calm under pressure and adapt quickly when plans change.

These experiences improved my problem-solving skills beyond programming.

---

## What I Learned

Through my leadership experiences, I've learned:

- Team management
- Communication
- Public speaking
- Event planning
- Community building
- Professional networking
- Time management
- Decision making
- Conflict resolution
- Responsibility and accountability

These skills have helped me become a better software developer because successful software projects also require effective teamwork and communication.

## Leadership Philosophy

I believe leadership is not about giving instructions but about helping people succeed together.

I enjoy supporting teammates, encouraging collaboration, and creating environments where everyone has opportunities to learn and contribute.

For me, good leadership means taking responsibility, leading by example, and continuously learning from every experience.

---

## Why Leadership Matters To Me

Leadership has helped me grow as a person, not just as a developer.

It has improved my confidence, communication skills, and ability to work with people from different backgrounds.

These experiences continue to influence how I approach both software projects and collaborative work, and I believe they will remain valuable throughout my career.


## What Motivates Me As A Leader

One of the most rewarding parts of leadership is seeing people benefit from something the team has built together.

Whether it's organizing a technical event, inviting an industry speaker, or creating platforms that make student activities easier, I enjoy contributing to initiatives that help others learn and grow.

For me, leadership is another way of creating impact beyond writing code.
